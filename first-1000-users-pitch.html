Platform Rules

4 platforms.
Distinct rules for each.

Reddit
Peer who's been through it
  • Lowercase 'i' throughout
  • No em dashes — ever
  • Product mention last sentence only
  • Human filler: 'tbh', 'honestly'
em dashes, marketing adjectives
Slack
Helpful colleague
  • 2–4 sentences max
  • Professional, not corporate
  • Never pitch in first message
  • DM only after channel reply
Same message across channels
X / Twitter
Opinionated practitioner
  • Reply or quote-tweet length only
  • One strong idea, no hedging
  • Product only if directly relevant
  • No reply-guy spam
DM without explicit invite
Hacker News
Technical peer with depth
  • Real technical depth required
  • Product: last line only
  • Comment must stand alone
  • Zero marketing language
"Great question!", buzzwords

Design Principles

Built on real lessons.
v1 to v5.1.

Every decision has a reason.

01
NO AUTOMATION

No automation — ever

Playwright auto-posting was built in v3 and fully removed in v4. Platform seeding works because it looks real. The moment automation enters, the strategy collapses.

03
ETHICS

DM ethics are non-negotiable

One DM per person. Always reference their specific post. Never follow up if no reply. Bulk DMs destroy the trust the entire strategy depends on.

02
ICP FIRST

ICP before everything

Founders write specs in marketing language. The AI must challenge this first — a vague ICP produces wrong communities, wrong signals, and ignored replies.

04
FEEDBACK LOOP

20 interactions = feedback trigger

10 is noise. 50 is 5 weeks — too long to pivot. 20 is exactly enough data to distinguish pattern from noise and trigger Check-in Mode.
